After the first month of being in a space shuttle with other people, which I have not mentioned, it feels like I have lost interest in this trip. Some of the fun parts that have happened we were actually able to land on and walk on certain moons and planets. Otherwise, it was usually an instructor who would talk to us about a certain asteroid, moon, or planet for an hour at a time. When there was free time, there was not much else to do except wait the next lecture about another ball of rock and gas. All we could do to pass time between the lectures was eat and sleep. I had high hopes for going to space, but now they are gone.

Unfamiliar Terms About Space

  • Kuiper Belt - a region of the solar system beyond the orbit of Neptune, believed to contain many comets, asteroids, and other small bodies made largely of ice
  • Heliosphere - a vast, bubble-like region of space that surrounds the solar system and is created by the Sun, its purpose is to shield the planets for interstellar radiation
  • Heliocentric -having or representing the sun as the center, as in the accepted astronomical model of the solar system
